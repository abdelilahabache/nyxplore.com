15 Best Free Skyline Views (That Aren't the Empire State)

By NYxplore Team May 16, 2025 8 min read

New York’s skyline is world-famous, but you don’t have to empty your wallet at an observation deck to enjoy it. In fact, some of the best views are completely free — and often less crowded. From iconic parks to under-the-radar rooftops, these 15 spots offer jaw-dropping perspectives of the city that never sleeps.

1. Brooklyn Heights Promenade

Enjoy sweeping views of Lower Manhattan, the Brooklyn Bridge, and the Statue of Liberty all from one elegant promenade lined with benches and trees.

2. Gantry Plaza State Park

Located in Long Island City, this waterfront park offers pristine views of Midtown Manhattan and the iconic Pepsi-Cola sign.

3. Roosevelt Island

Ride the tram for the cost of a subway swipe and enjoy unique views as you float above the East River. Walk south to Franklin D. Roosevelt Four Freedoms Park for panoramic vistas.

4. Domino Park

Set along the Williamsburg waterfront, this modern park gives you incredible skyline views backed by industrial-chic design elements.

5. The High Line

This elevated park weaves through the West Side with multiple spots to catch glimpses of the city, the Hudson River, and beyond.

6. DUMBO’s Washington Street

The perfect photo op: the Manhattan Bridge perfectly framed with the Empire State Building peeking through.

7. Hunter’s Point South Park

Another gem in Queens, this park is quiet, beautifully designed, and offers crystal-clear views of Manhattan’s east side skyline.

8. Staten Island Ferry

Completely free and running 24/7, this ferry gives you one of the best views of Lower Manhattan and the Statue of Liberty.

9. Transmitter Park

Located in Greenpoint, Brooklyn, this small park feels local and relaxed, with clear skyline views across the river.

10. Fort Tryon Park

Overlooking the Hudson River, this Upper Manhattan park offers a more tranquil skyline experience from afar.

11. Governor’s Island

Open seasonally, this car-free island has sprawling lawns and overlooks offering rare, wide-angle skyline views.

12. Bushwick Inlet Park

Part park, part hidden secret — come for the waterfront, stay for the peaceful views of Midtown.

13. Carl Schurz Park

Quiet, scenic, and nestled on the Upper East Side, this park offers bridges-and-river skyline views with fewer crowds.

14. Liberty State Park (NJ)

Just across the river in Jersey City, this park gives you a postcard view of Lower Manhattan with room to stretch out.

15. Astoria Park

Known for its historic pool and epic views of the RFK and Hell Gate Bridges, it also offers a quiet skyline snapshot from the north.

Skip the long lines and ticket fees. With these free spots, you’ll experience NYC’s stunning skyline like a true local — one breathtaking view at a time.
