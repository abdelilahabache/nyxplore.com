Subway System

The fastest way to get around NYC with 24/7 service on most lines. Over 5 million riders daily.

Download the MYmta app for real-time updates and service changes.

Key Subway Lines:

1 2 3 West Side (Manhattan to Bronx/Brooklyn)

4 5 6 East Side (Manhattan to Bronx)

N Q R Broadway (Manhattan to Queens/Brooklyn)

Bus Network

Comprehensive coverage with over 300 routes serving all five boroughs.

Use the Bus Time feature in the MYmta app to track buses in real-time.

Select Bus Service (SBS):

  • Faster service with off-board fare payment
  • Limited stops and dedicated lanes
  • Routes include M15, B44, Bx12, Q44, and more

Express Buses:

Premium service between boroughs and Manhattan with comfortable seating.

Citi Bike & Cycling

Over 1,500 bike share stations and 1,000+ miles of bike lanes across NYC.

Get unlimited 30-minute rides with a Citi Bike Day Pass ($15).

Citi Bike Options:

  • Single ride: $3.50 + tax
  • Day pass: $15 (unlimited 30-min rides)
  • Annual membership: $205 (best for locals)

Best Bike Routes:

  • Hudson River Greenway
  • Brooklyn Waterfront Greenway
  • Central Park Loop

NYC Ferry

Scenic and efficient waterway transportation with 6 routes connecting the boroughs.

Ferries accept MetroCards and have bike racks onboard.

Ferry Routes:

  • East River (Lower Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens)
  • South Brooklyn (Bay Ridge to Wall St)
  • Rockaway (Wall St to Rockaway Beach)
  • Astoria (Wall St to Astoria)

Fares:

Same as subway: $2.90 per ride, free transfers within 2 hours.

Walking NYC

Manhattan is only 2.3 miles wide - many destinations are walkable!

Manhattan streets are numbered - odd=west, even=east from 5th Ave.

Walking Times:

  • 20 blocks = 1 mile
  • Average block: 1 min walk (north-south), 3 min walk (east-west)
  • Times Square to Central Park: 15 min walk

Pedestrian Tips:

  • Look both ways - one-way streets can be confusing
  • Use crosswalks and wait for walk signals
  • Download a walking map or offline directions

Fares & MetroCard

One payment system for subway, buses, and ferries.

Get unlimited rides with 7-day ($33) or 30-day ($127) unlimited MetroCards.

Payment Options:

  • Single ride: $2.90 (no transfers)
  • Pay-Per-Ride MetroCard: $2.90 with free transfers
  • Unlimited 7-day: $33 (best for visitors)
  • OMNY contactless payment (credit/debit cards, phones, watches)

Where to Buy:

  • Subway station vending machines
  • Convenience stores with MetroCard sign
  • OMNY readers at all subway stations and buses
