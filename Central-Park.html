Central Park

New York's 843-Acre Urban Oasis

An Urban Masterpiece

Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux in 1858, Central Park is the most visited urban park in the United States with over 42 million visitors annually.

Iconic Bridges

The park features 36 ornamental bridges, each with unique designs. The most famous is Bow Bridge, a cast iron bridge spanning 60 feet across the Lake.

Central Park Zoo

A 6.5-acre zoo located near the southeast corner of the park. Home to over 130 species including snow leopards, penguins, and grizzly bears.

Belvedere Castle

A folly in Central Park offering one of the highest vantage points in the park with panoramic views of the Great Lawn and Turtle Pond.

Visitor Tips

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (6-8am) for fewer crowds and soft light. Sunset offers beautiful views but more visitors. Weekdays are generally less crowded than weekends.

What to Bring

Comfortable walking shoes, water, sunscreen, and a camera. The park is much larger than it appears on maps!

Getting Around

The park is easily walkable, but bike rentals are available. Pedicabs offer guided tours. Subway stations surround the park at all sides.

Historical Timeline

1858

Design competition won by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux with their "Greensward Plan"

1873

Construction completed after 15 years and $10 million (equivalent to $200 million today)

1963

Designated a National Historic Landmark by the U.S. National Park Service

1980

Central Park Conservancy founded to restore and manage the park
