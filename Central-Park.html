Central Park

New York's Urban Oasis

843 Acres of Natural Beauty

Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux in 1858

Central Park is an urban park in New York City located between the Upper West and Upper East Sides of Manhattan. It is the fifth-largest park in the city by area, covering 843 acres. It is the most visited urban park in the United States, with an estimated 42 million visitors annually.

Iconic Bridges

The park features 36 ornamental bridges, each with unique designs. The most famous is Bow Bridge, a cast iron bridge spanning 60 feet across the Lake.

Central Park Zoo

A 6.5-acre zoo located near the southeast corner of the park. Home to over 130 species including snow leopards, penguins, and grizzly bears.

Belvedere Castle

A folly in Central Park offering one of the highest vantage points in the park with panoramic views of the Great Lawn and Turtle Pond.
