Brooklyn Bridge

Connecting Manhattan and Brooklyn since 1883

An Engineering Marvel

The first steel-wire suspension bridge in the world

The Brooklyn Bridge is a hybrid cable-stayed/suspension bridge in New York City, spanning the East River between the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn. When opened in 1883, it was the longest suspension bridge in the world with a main span of 1,595.5 feet.

Walking Across the Bridge

1

Start in Manhattan

Begin at City Hall Park in Manhattan. The pedestrian entrance is near the intersection of Centre Street and Chambers Street.

2

Walk the Promenade

The wooden walkway is elevated above the car traffic, offering spectacular views of both Manhattan and Brooklyn.

3

Arrive in Brooklyn

End your walk in DUMBO (Down Under the Manhattan Bridge Overpass), a trendy neighborhood with great dining options.

Best Photo Spots

Manhattan Side

City Hall Park offers classic shots of the bridge's stone arches.

Mid-Span

The middle of the bridge provides panoramic views of both boroughs.

Brooklyn Side

Brooklyn Bridge Park offers postcard-perfect views of the bridge and Manhattan skyline.

Historical Timeline

1869

Construction begins under John A. Roebling's design

1883

Bridge opens to the public on May 24 after 14 years of construction

1964

Designated a National Historic Landmark

1983

Centennial celebration with fireworks and special events
