When NYC Museums Are Free (Or Pay-What-You-Wish)

By NYxplore Team May 14, 2025 10 min read
Food Pizza Local Favorites Neighborhood Guides

New York City is a treasure trove of world-class museums, ranging from cutting-edge contemporary art spaces to vast natural history collections. While visiting these cultural landmarks can sometimes come with a hefty price tag, many museums offer free or pay-what-you-wish admission options that make them more accessible to everyone. Whether you're a local looking for a budget-friendly day out or a visitor eager to dive into NYC’s rich artistic and historical heritage, these opportunities are well worth exploring.

Plan ahead to take advantage of these special entry times and experience the city’s incredible museum scene without breaking the bank.

1. The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA)

MoMA stands as a beacon for modern and contemporary art lovers worldwide, featuring masterpieces from Picasso, Warhol, and many others. Every Friday from 5:30 PM to 9 PM, admission is free, making it a popular evening spot for both locals and tourists. Arriving early is recommended as this time slot can get crowded. Beyond its iconic paintings, the museum also hosts film screenings, talks, and special exhibitions.

Pro Tip: After your visit, grab a coffee or snack at the museum café, which offers stunning views of Midtown Manhattan.

2. The American Museum of Natural History

This museum invites visitors to pay what they wish for general admission tickets, a unique policy that has allowed millions to explore its vast halls of dinosaur fossils, gems, space exhibits, and cultural artifacts. Situated on the Upper West Side, it’s a great family-friendly destination. Don’t miss the Rose Center for Earth and Space or the Butterfly Conservatory when in season.

Keep in mind, special exhibitions and the Hayden Planetarium may require separate tickets.

3. The Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum

Famous for its spiraling Frank Lloyd Wright-designed building, the Guggenheim offers pay-what-you-wish admission every Saturday from 5 PM to 8 PM. It’s an ideal time to see impressive collections of abstract, modern, and contemporary art. The museum’s architecture alone is worth the visit, providing visitors with a unique way to experience art as they ascend the ramped galleries.

Tip: Take your time to admire both the art and the building’s design — the open rotunda is a marvel in itself.

4. The Brooklyn Museum

One of the largest and most diverse art museums in the country, the Brooklyn Museum offers free admission on the first Saturday of each month (except September). These evenings often include live music, workshops, and talks, creating a vibrant cultural atmosphere. The museum’s collections span ancient Egyptian artifacts to contemporary art, providing something for everyone.

Nearby, you can explore the Brooklyn Botanic Garden or the bustling neighborhoods of Prospect Heights and Park Slope afterward.

5. The Whitney Museum of American Art

The Whitney showcases an impressive collection of 20th and 21st-century American art, with a focus on living artists. Every Friday from 7 PM to 10 PM, the museum welcomes visitors with a pay-what-you-wish admission policy. Located in the vibrant Meatpacking District, the museum also hosts film screenings, performances, and special exhibitions that reflect contemporary American culture.

After your visit, take a stroll along the nearby High Line, an elevated park offering views of the Hudson River and city skyline.

6. The New Museum

This innovative museum dedicated to contemporary art offers pay-what-you-wish admission on Thursdays from 7 PM to 9 PM. Located in the Lower East Side, it focuses on emerging artists and experimental projects that push boundaries. Its cutting-edge exhibitions provide a fresh perspective on current cultural and social issues.

7. The Bronx Museum of the Arts

With a commitment to showcasing diverse artists, especially those from the Bronx community, this museum offers free admission year-round. It’s a fantastic place to explore contemporary art, multimedia installations, and cultural history in an often overlooked borough of NYC.

Tips for Making the Most of Your Visit

  • Check Official Websites: Museum hours and pay-what-you-wish/free admission times can change seasonally or for special events. Confirm before you go.
  • Arrive Early: Free or discounted times are popular, so arriving early can help you avoid long lines and get the most out of your visit.
  • Plan Your Route: Many museums are near great dining spots, parks, and other attractions, so consider combining your visit with nearby experiences.
  • Consider Memberships: If you plan to visit multiple museums or return often, memberships can be a cost-effective way to support the arts while enjoying perks like early access.
  • Respect the Rules: Some museums may restrict photography or food inside galleries—be sure to follow guidelines to preserve the experience for everyone.

Exploring NYC’s museums through free and pay-what-you-wish programs is a fantastic way to connect with the city’s vibrant arts scene without the usual expense. From timeless classics to groundbreaking contemporary works, these cultural institutions offer something for everyone.
