The Definitive NYC Pizza Ranking: 15 Must-Try Slices

By NYxplore Team May 14, 2025 10 min read
Food Pizza Local Favorites Neighborhood Guides

New York City's pizza scene is legendary, with thousands of pizzerias serving slices that range from classic dollar slices to gourmet creations. After months of research (and many, many slices), we've compiled the definitive ranking of NYC's 15 best pizza slices.

From Brooklyn to the Bronx, these are the slices every pizza lover should try at least once. We've considered crust, sauce, cheese, toppings, and that intangible "New York factor" that makes a slice truly special.

What Makes NYC Pizza Special?

Before we dive into our rankings, let's talk about what sets New York pizza apart:

  • The Water: NYC's tap water creates a unique dough texture that's hard to replicate elsewhere
  • The Ovens: Many classic spots still use the same coal or brick ovens for decades
  • The Fold: The perfect NYC slice has just the right balance to support the iconic fold
  • The Attitude: It's fast, it's cheap (sometimes), and it's always satisfying

The 15 Best Pizza Slices in NYC

1. Di Fara Pizza - Midwood, Brooklyn

1424 Avenue J Q to Avenue J $5-$7 per slice

The late Dom DeMarco's legendary pies are worth the wait (and the price). Each slice gets a drizzle of olive oil, fresh basil, and a blend of cheeses grated right onto the pizza. The square slice is particularly famous.

Must-Try: The classic round slice with fresh mozzarella and basil

2. Joe's Pizza - Greenwich Village

7 Carmine St A, B, C, D, E, F, M to W 4th St $3.50-$4.50 per slice

This no-frills spot serves what many consider the quintessential NYC slice. Perfectly crisp yet foldable crust, tangy sauce, and just the right cheese ratio make this a benchmark for classic NY pizza.

Must-Try: The plain cheese slice - it doesn't get more classic than this

3. Lucali - Carroll Gardens, Brooklyn

575 Henry St F, G to Carroll St Whole pies only (~$30)

Mark Iacono's candlelit pizzeria makes arguably the most beautiful pies in NYC. The thin crust with its leopard-spotted char supports simple, high-quality toppings. BYOB and prepare to wait.

Must-Try: The basil pie with fresh garlic and extra cheese

4. Scarr's Pizza - Lower East Side

35 Orchard St F to Delancey St $4-$6 per slice

Scarr's stone-grounds its own flour for dough with exceptional texture and flavor. The retro vibe and attention to detail make this one of NYC's best new-old-school pizzerias.

Must-Try: The pepperoni slice with 'nduja honey

5. L'Industrie Pizzeria - Williamsburg, Brooklyn

254 S 2nd St J, M to Marcy Ave $4-$7 per slice

Massimo Laveglia's tiny shop serves slices with perfect balance - crisp yet tender crust, bright sauce, and inventive toppings. The burrata slice is legendary among pizza connoisseurs.

Must-Try: The burrata slice with fresh basil

6. Patsy's Pizzeria - East Harlem

2287 1st Ave 6 to 116th St $3.50-$5 per slice

This 1933 coal-oven institution serves thin-crust pies with the perfect char. Frank Sinatra's favorite pizza still uses the original recipe - simple, classic, and utterly delicious.

Must-Try: The classic margherita slice

7. Best Pizza - Williamsburg, Brooklyn

33 Havemeyer St L to Bedford Ave $3.50-$5 per slice

Frank Pinello's spot lives up to its bold name with perfect execution of NYC classics. The white slice with garlic and romano is one of Brooklyn's best-kept secrets.

Must-Try: The white slice with fresh garlic

8. Prince Street Pizza - Nolita

27 Prince St N, R to Prince St $4-$6 per slice

Famous for their spicy square slices, Prince Street's pepperoni cups crisp into little bowls of spicy oil. The contrast between crispy edges and soft center is textbook-perfect.

Must-Try: The Spicy Spring square slice

9. Totonno's - Coney Island, Brooklyn

1524 Neptune Ave D, F, N, Q to Coney Island-Stillwell Ave Whole pies only (~$25)

This 1924 coal-oven legend survived Sandy and a fire to keep serving some of NYC's most balanced pies. The crust has just the right chew and the sauce sings with bright tomato flavor.

Must-Try: The classic mozzarella and sausage pie

10. Roberta's - Bushwick, Brooklyn

261 Moore St L to Morgan Ave Whole pies only (~$18-$24)

The wood-fired pies at this hipster haven put Bushwick on the pizza map. Creative toppings meet perfect crust in this industrial-chic space that's become a neighborhood institution.

Must-Try: The Famous Original with pepperoni, onions, and jalape√±os

11. John's of Bleecker Street - West Village

278 Bleecker St 1 to Christopher St Whole pies only (~$25)

Since 1929, this coal-oven temple has been serving blistered, thin-crust pies in a space covered with decades of carved graffiti. No slices means you'll need friends (or leftovers).

Must-Try: The classic margherita with fresh basil

12. Paulie Gee's - Greenpoint, Brooklyn

60 Greenpoint Ave G to Greenpoint Ave Whole pies only (~$20-$25)

Artisanal toppings meet perfect wood-fired crust at this hip Greenpoint spot. The creative pies (like the Hellboy with spicy honey) have made this a destination for pizza pilgrims.

Must-Try: The Hellboy with soppressata and Mike's Hot Honey

13. L&B Spumoni Gardens - Bensonhurst, Brooklyn

2725 86th St D to 25th Ave $4-$6 per slice

The iconic square slice here features sweet sauce on top of the cheese - a Bensonhurst tradition. The crisp-bottomed, fluffy Sicilian-style slices pair perfectly with their namesake spumoni.

Must-Try: The square slice (and spumoni for dessert)

14. Una Pizza Napoletana - Lower East Side

175 Orchard St F to Delancey St Whole pies only (~$25)

Anthony Mangieri's temple to Neapolitan pizza serves what many consider the most authentic version outside Naples. The minimalist menu focuses on perfection of basics with 00 flour and San Marzano tomatoes.

Must-Try: The marinara pizza with just tomato, garlic, oregano and olive oil

15. Artichoke Basille's Pizza - Multiple Locations

Original location: 328 E 14th St L to 1st Ave $5-$8 per slice

The rich, creamy artichoke slice has achieved cult status among NYC pizza lovers. While they serve excellent traditional slices, it's the namesake artichoke-spinach slice that draws lines down the block.

Must-Try: The signature artichoke slice (share it - it's rich!)

NYC Pizza Tips & Etiquette

To enjoy NYC pizza like a local, keep these tips in mind:

1. Know When to Fold

A properly balanced NYC slice should be foldable - this isn't just tradition, it's structural engineering. Fold lengthwise to eat without losing toppings.

2. Dollar Slice Rules

At dollar slice joints, order and pay first, then take your receipt to the counter. And yes, they really are still $1 at many places (though $1.50 is becoming common).

3. Toppings Philosophy

Classic NYC pizzerias keep toppings simple - pepperoni, mushrooms, sausage. Gourmet spots get creative, but purists believe a great pizza needs nothing more than great dough, sauce and cheese.

4. Reheating Leftovers

The best way to reheat NYC pizza is in a dry skillet over medium heat - no microwave! This restores crispness to the crust.
