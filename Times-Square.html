Times Square

The Crossroads of the World

The Heart of New York City

Where bright lights, Broadway shows, and the energy of NYC come together

Times Square is one of the world's most visited tourist attractions, drawing over 50 million visitors annually. Named after the New York Times in 1904 when the newspaper moved its headquarters to the newly built Times Building, this iconic intersection has become synonymous with New York City's vibrant energy.

Times Square Experiences

Daytime Exploration

Discover flagship stores, street performers, and architectural details that shine in daylight. Visit the TKTS booth for same-day Broadway tickets at discounted prices.

Nighttime Magic

Experience the transformation as neon lights illuminate the square. The energy peaks at night with live performances and the famous New Year's Eve ball drop location.

Broadway Theaters

Home to 41 professional theaters hosting world-class performances from classic musicals to cutting-edge plays. Many offer rush and lottery tickets for same-day shows.

Historical Timeline

1904

Originally called Longacre Square, it was renamed Times Square after the New York Times moved its headquarters to the newly built Times Building.

1907

The first New Year's Eve ball drop celebration took place, starting an annual tradition that continues to this day.

1920s-1960s

Times Square became the center of New York's theater district and entertainment industry, though it also developed a reputation for vice and crime.

1990s

Major redevelopment transformed Times Square into a family-friendly tourist destination with new attractions and corporate headquarters.

Visitor Tips

Best Time to Visit

Early morning (7-9am) for fewer crowds. Evenings offer the full neon experience but can be extremely crowded. Weekdays are generally less busy than weekends.

Getting There

Multiple subway lines serve Times Square (1/2/3, N/Q/R/W, 7, A/C/E). The area is pedestrian-friendly but be mindful of vehicle traffic when crossing streets.

Safety Tips

Stay aware of your surroundings, keep valuables secure, and be cautious of street hustlers. Official costumed characters should display identification.
