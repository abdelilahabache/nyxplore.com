The Heart of American Theater

Broadway is synonymous with theatrical excellence, hosting some of the most spectacular productions in the world. With over 40 professional theaters in the Theater District and Lincoln Center along Broadway in Midtown Manhattan, this is where dreams are made and stars are born.

Broadway Visitor Tips

Getting Tickets

Learn about TKTS booths (discount same-day tickets), rush tickets (discounted tickets sold when the box office opens), lottery tickets, and online discounts. The TKTS booth in Times Square offers same-day tickets for up to 50% off.

Theater Etiquette

Arrive at least 30 minutes early, silence phones, avoid talking during performances, and dress appropriately (business casual is typical). Photography and recording are strictly prohibited.

Dining Nearby

Great pre-theater dining options include Sardi's (legendary theater district restaurant), Joe Allen (popular with actors), and Glass House Tavern. Make reservations for 5:30-6:30pm for 8pm shows.

A Rich Theatrical History

Broadway theater dates back to the mid-18th century when the first theaters opened in New York City. The Theater District as we know it today began to take shape in the early 20th century, with the construction of many theaters that are still in use today.

The term "Broadway" originally referred to the physical street, but became synonymous with professional theater when the majority of theaters were built near the street. The first musical comedy on Broadway was "The Black Crook" in 1866, running for a then-unprecedented 474 performances.

Today, Broadway is considered the highest level of commercial theater in the English-speaking world, with shows often transferring to London's West End and touring productions across the globe.

Accessibility Information

Wheelchair Access

Most Broadway theaters offer wheelchair seating and accessible restrooms. Call the theater box office in advance to arrange accommodations.

Assisted Listening

Many theaters provide assistive listening devices. Some offer sign language interpreted and open caption performances on specific dates.

Autism-Friendly

Select shows offer autism-friendly performances with adjusted lighting and sound, and a more relaxed environment.
