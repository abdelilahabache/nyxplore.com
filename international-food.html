Global Flavors in New York

New York City offers an unparalleled culinary journey with authentic restaurants representing every corner of the globe. Here's where to find the world's best cuisines right here in NYC.

#1: Moroccan Cuisine in NYC

Experience the flavors of Morocco without leaving New York City. The best Moroccan restaurants can be found in the Diamond District (Midtown) and Atlantic Avenue (Brooklyn). These establishments transport diners to Marrakech with their authentic tagines, couscous, and mint tea service.

From the ornate decor to the traditional floor seating options, NYC's Moroccan restaurants offer a complete cultural immersion along with their celebrated cuisine.

#1 in the World

Moroccan in NYC

Where to find authentic Moroccan cuisine in New York:

  • Midtown: Café Mogador (East Village), Dar Yema (Diamond District)
  • Brooklyn: Bedouin Tent (Atlantic Ave), Zaytoons (Park Slope)
  • Best for: Tagines at Café Marrakesh (Midtown West)
  • Hidden gem: Tagine Dining (West Village) for private dining experience

Why Moroccan Cuisine Stands Out in NYC

New York's Moroccan restaurants offer a unique dining experience that combines authentic flavors with the city's vibrant energy. Chef Ahmed Benbrahim of Dar Yema explains: "We import many ingredients directly from Morocco - the saffron from Taliouine, argan oil from Essaouira, and our spices come from the markets of Marrakech."

What makes NYC's Moroccan dining scene special is the variety - from casual couscous spots in Brooklyn to elegant Midtown establishments with belly dancing performances. Many restaurants source ingredients from local halal butchers and Middle Eastern markets in Brooklyn and Queens.

The communal aspect of Moroccan dining translates well to New York's social dining culture. "New Yorkers love sharing plates, which is perfect for our tagines and mezze," says owner Leila Mourad of Café Mogador.

NYC International Dining Tips

Finding Authentic International Cuisines

1) Look for restaurants in ethnic enclaves (Jackson Heights for Indian, Flushing for Chinese). 2) Check if the menu offers regional specialties. 3) Visit during traditional meal times when regulars dine. 4) Look for imported ingredients on the menu.

Best Neighborhoods for Global Food

1) Flushing: Best Chinese outside China. 2) Jackson Heights: South Asian and Latin American. 3) Brighton Beach: Russian/Ukrainian. 4) Sunset Park: Mexican and Chinese. 5) Arthur Ave: Authentic Italian.

Affordable International Eats

1) $1 dumplings in Chinatown. 2) $5 arepas in Jackson Heights. 3) $10 lunch specials at Korean spots in K-Town. 4) $15 prix-fixe at Indian lunch buffets. 5) $20 Ethiopian platters in Harlem.

International Food Markets

1) Kalustyan's (Middle Eastern/Indian). 2) Patel Brothers (Indian). 3) H Mart (Korean). 4) NetCost Market (Russian). 5) Hong Kong Supermarket (Chinese). 6) Sahadi's (Middle Eastern).
