Statue of Liberty

A universal symbol of freedom and democracy

Lady Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbor. Designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and built by Gustave Eiffel, the statue was dedicated on October 28, 1886, as a gift from France to the United States.

Fascinating Facts

Construction

The statue was constructed in France, shipped overseas in crates, and assembled on the completed pedestal on what was then called Bedloe's Island.

Symbolism

The seven spikes on the crown represent the seven oceans and seven continents, indicating universal liberty. The tablet in her left hand reads "July 4, 1776" in Roman numerals.

Restoration

For its centennial in 1986, the statue underwent a major restoration that included replacing the torch (now covered in gold leaf) and strengthening the internal structure.

Visitor Information

Getting There

The only way to access Liberty Island is by ferry. Statue Cruises is the official ferry service provider and departs from Battery Park in Manhattan and Liberty State Park in New Jersey.

  • Ferries run daily (except December 25)
  • First ferry departs at 8:30am
  • Last ferry departs at 3:30pm
  • Security screening required

Ticket Options

There are several ticket options available depending on what you'd like to experience:

  • Grounds Ticket: Access to Liberty Island and Ellis Island
  • Pedestal Ticket: Includes access to the pedestal observation deck
  • Crown Ticket: Limited access to the crown (must climb 354 steps)
  • Hard Hat Tour: Guided tour of Ellis Island Hospital
