Transportation

What's the best way to get around NYC?

The subway is the fastest and most affordable way to get around NYC, operating 24/7. For shorter distances, walking is often the best option in Manhattan. Taxis and rideshares (Uber/Lyft) are convenient but more expensive, especially during peak hours. Buses are good for crosstown routes where subways don't run.

Consider getting a MetroCard (or using OMNY contactless payment) for subway and bus access. Unlimited ride passes are available for 7 or 30 days if you'll be using public transit frequently.

How do I get from JFK to Manhattan?

There are several options:

  • AirTrain + Subway: ($8.25) Take AirTrain to Jamaica Station (for E, J, Z trains) or Howard Beach (for A train), then transfer to subway
  • Taxi: Flat rate of $52 (plus tolls and tip) to Manhattan
  • Rideshare: Uber/Lyft typically cost $45-$75 depending on demand
  • Airport Shuttle: Shared vans like NYC Airporter cost about $20 per person
  • Private Car: Services like Dial7 or Carmel cost $75-$100
Is the NYC subway safe at night?

Generally yes, especially on busy lines and in Manhattan. However, it's wise to take precautions:

  • Wait near other people or near the conductor's area
  • Stay in well-lit cars (usually the middle cars)
  • Be aware of your surroundings
  • Avoid empty stations late at night
  • Consider taking a taxi if you're uncomfortable

Crime rates on the subway are relatively low, but petty theft does occur, so keep valuables secure.

Attractions

What are the must-see attractions for first-time visitors?

Top attractions for first-timers include:

  • Times Square: The iconic crossroads of the world (best visited at night)
  • Central Park: 843 acres of green space with many points of interest
  • Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island: Book ferry tickets in advance
  • Empire State Building or Top of the Rock: Stunning city views
  • Metropolitan Museum of Art: One of the world's great museums
  • Broadway Show: See a world-class theater production
  • 9/11 Memorial & Museum: Powerful tribute to 9/11 victims

Consider a CityPASS or similar attraction pass if visiting multiple paid attractions.

Are there any free attractions in NYC?

Yes! NYC has many excellent free attractions:

  • Museums: Many offer free hours (MoMA - Fridays 4-8pm, AMNH - pay-what-you-wish)
  • Parks: Central Park, High Line, Brooklyn Bridge Park
  • Landmarks: Grand Central Terminal, New York Public Library, Charging Bull
  • Ferries: Staten Island Ferry (free with great Statue of Liberty views)
  • Neighborhoods: Walk through Chinatown, Little Italy, Greenwich Village
  • Outdoor Events: SummerStage concerts, Shakespeare in the Park (free tickets)
How early should I arrive for Broadway shows?

For Broadway shows:

  • Arrive at least 30 minutes before curtain time
  • If picking up tickets at will call, arrive 45 minutes early
  • Latecomers may not be seated until intermission
  • Allow extra time if dining beforehand (restaurants near theaters get crowded)
  • Weekend evenings are busiest - allow more time

Most shows start promptly at the scheduled time (8pm for evening shows, 2pm for matinees).

Dining

Do I need to make restaurant reservations in advance?

It depends on the restaurant and when you're visiting:

  • Popular restaurants: Book 2-4 weeks in advance (some require 1 month+)
  • Weekends: Always make reservations if possible
  • Lunch: Often easier to walk in than dinner
  • Off-hours: Try dining early (5-6pm) or late (after 9pm)
  • Alternatives: Many places have bar seating for walk-ins

Use apps like Resy or OpenTable to find availability. Some famous spots (like Katz's Deli) don't take reservations.

What's the tipping etiquette in NYC restaurants?

Standard tipping in NYC:

  • Restaurants: 18-20% of pre-tax bill (15% is considered minimum)
  • Bartenders: $1-2 per drink or 15-20% of tab
  • Delivery: 15-20% of order ($2-5 minimum)
  • Taxi/Rideshare: 15-20% of fare
  • Hotel staff: $1-2 per bag for bellhops, $2-5 per night for housekeeping

Many restaurants now include suggested tip amounts on receipts. Credit cards are accepted everywhere for tipping.
